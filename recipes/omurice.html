Omurice

japanese omelette with a spiced filling

Omurice is a famous japanese dish especially liked among children. The base of Omurice is
consisting of a mixture of vegetables, meat and spices. A sauce of ketchup and other ingredients
give this dish a light sweet flavor.

The name Omurice comes from two words. Omu (omuretsu) meaning omelette and Rice (raisu) meaning rice
and the name explains the dish very well already!

Are your taste buds ready?

Ingredients (one serving)

  • 2 cups of rice
  • 50g of Chicken
  • 1/2 Onion
  • 5 Tbsp Ketchup
  • 1 Tbsp Butter
  • 2 Tbsp of oil (neutral-tasting)
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Tbsp Milk
  • 1/2 a handful of peas
  • 1/2 a handful of carrots
  • 1/2 a handful of corn

Steps

  1. First cook the rice in a rice cooker, in a pot or use leftover rice from the previous day.
  2. Cut the onions and the chicken into fine cubes.
  3. Sauté the onions until they are translucent. Only then add the chicken.
  4. When the meat is well-done, add the cooked rice, peas, corn and carrots.
  5. Spice everything with a bit of salt and pepper
  6. Let the rice continue to cook. Meanwhile add the Eggs into a bowl and mix them with the cheese, milk and some salt.
  7. When the rice is well-done, mix it well with the ketchup. The filling is done now.
  8. Take a clean pan, and heat it with Oil.
  9. Put the egg-mixture into the hot pan.
  10. As soon as the egg is set, the omelette is done. If you prefer you can have it in there for a maximum of one or two additional minutes
  11. Put the omelette onto a flat plate, put the rice mixture on one half then fold the other half over it. You're done! If you want, server it with some Ketchup on top!
